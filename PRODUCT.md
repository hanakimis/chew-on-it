# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A single learner using short, focused sessions to build practical judgment about frontend development and visual design.

## Product Purpose

Chew-on-it turns broad subjects into small, interactive lessons. Each lesson should make one idea observable, explain where it is useful, and surface the tradeoffs so the learner can apply it rather than merely recall it.

## Brand Personality

Practical, useful, scientific. The voice is direct and curious, with the rigor of a good experiment and none of the posture of a textbook.

## Anti-references

Avoid textbook chapters, long-form articles disguised as courses, passive design galleries, ornamental explanations, and lessons that state rules without demonstrating their consequences.

## Design Principles

- Make one idea visible in each lesson.
- Prefer a small experiment over a long explanation.
- Explain tradeoffs, not commandments.
- Keep the learner moving in short sessions.
- Use precise, plain language.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal conformance target is required. Preserve semantic HTML, keyboard operation, readable contrast, and reduced-motion behavior as sensible product defaults.
